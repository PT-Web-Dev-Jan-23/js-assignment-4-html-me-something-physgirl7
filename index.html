Over the years, my family has been able to take several vacations to take in the sights of the United States. We've visited many national parks and momuments; we've seen mountains and enormously tall trees; we've seen waterfalls, lakes, geysers, and wild animals. But, we've also gotten to see our nation's capitol and major cities like San Francisco and New York. We've traveled on subways and buses; we've seen incredibly tall buildings and incredibly ornate buildings as well as buildings and rooms of great importance. We've seen cathedrals as well as musicals and orchestras. Our incredibly diverse nation offers so much for us to see and experience.

The Grand Tetons, Yellowstone National Park, Mount Rushmore

Duration: 1 week

For our trip to these parks, we decided to drive. Since we live in St. Louis, MO, it was a pretty long drive to get there. We drove through Kansas City and then into Nebraska. Turns out Nebraska is a very long state to drive through! Our first stop was the Grand Tetons. Although it's a much smaller mountain chain that the Rocky Mountains, it is majestic. The first sight of these mountains was awe inspiring especially for people who rarely get to see mountains!

We also got to see a moose which was so much bigger in person than I had imagined. Luckily he was on river bank opposite us so we were far enough away to enjoy seeing him without provoking him.

Not far from the Grand Tetons is Yellowstone National Park. There was so much to take in at Yellowstone. The geysers, hot springs and paint pots are other-wordly and beautiful!

After spending a few days at Yellowstone we headed to Devils Tower. Devils tower stands out completely from it's surroundings. Just a large rock jutting out of the ground. It was formed by ancient molten lava flows. And, it's had many names as far back as we know. Other than Devils Tower, it's most common name is Bear Lodge; the name it was called by native American tribes. Devils tower has great cultural significance to several native American tribes.

From there we traveled to Mt. Rushmore. And then, we drove back home.

Grand Canyon, Painted Desert, Petrified Forest, Antelope Canyon, Arches National Park, Mesa Verde

Duration: 10 days

This trip included as much as we could pack in! We flew into Phoenix and from there rented a car to get us all around to these sites. We started by going to the Painted Desert National Park, a peaceful, quiet and serene place.

In the same area we also got to see the Petrified Forest National Park; it looks so barren and the petrified wood looks like wood, but is really fossilized by water rich in minerals. Essentially it's now a rock. You can learn more about this geologic process through the Wikipedia entry.

From the Petrified Forest we drove to The Grand Canyon. What can one say? It's the Grand Canyon. It's enormous and deep and in places terrifying. There are no guardrails here. Better watch where you stand! But, just awe inspiring. It makes one feel very small next to something so huge!

Next stop was Antelope Canyon. We had not planned this as a stop, but came across it when we were traveling and it may be one of the most stunningly beautiful places I've ever seen. You could literally spend all day here and it would look different at different times of day. Antelope canyon is a slot canyon. You can't see it above ground because while the canyon is open, the opening is thin, like a slot. Just take a look at these pics!

Another drive and we made it to Arches National Park. We took the Fiery Furnace tour which was more demanding than we anticipated but was also amazing! The arches are really stunning and the other rock formations are similarly amazing!

Our last stop was Mesa Verde. It's an interesting place to see in pictures, but even more so in real life. To see it up close involves taking a small hike in, but to get back out requires climbing some tall large ladders. To be there is to feel like you are in an ancient place. You can envision the life of a community there. In fact Mesa Verde is so special that it's been named a World Heritage Site.

Giant Sequoia National Monument, Yosemite National Park, Route 1 California, Monterey Bay Aquarium, San Francisco

Duration: 10 Days

This vacation covered a lot of places to see as well. Given that we had to drive a lot once we got to California, we opted to fly from St. Louis to San Francisco to begin our trip! Our first stop after landing is one of our favorite west coast eateries - In N Out Burger! Our first experience with In N Out Burger was when we landed in Phoenix for our last vacation and we loved it! Once we fueled up from our flight, we headed directly for Yosemite National Park. It was a jam packed drive out of the city but we eventually made it! Turns out that you can't really stay in a hotel very close to Yosemite unless you are staying in the park itself - and that takes reservations months if not years out. So, we stayed about an hour from the entrance. The drive to Yosemite each morning and evening was a little terrifying. The road was very twisty with a lot of deep dropoffs on the side of the road. But, Yosemite was beautiful. We got to see El Capitan, Half Dome, and several waterfalls. It was breathtaking and exhilarating and sometimes scary.

After a couple of days at Yosemite, we traveled a little further to reach Giant Sequoia National Park. When I was planning this vacation, I thought this would be an interesting park but that it wouldn't necessarily be a highlight of the overall vacation. It's hard to put into words what being amongst the giant sequoias feels like. The experience was sublime almost spiritual. Never have I felt so small and yet surrounded by so much life. It really was one of the highlights of the trip.

Now, heading back toward San Francisco, we took route 1 along the California coast. Much of the time the highway runs right along the coast of the Pacific Ocean. One of the most beautiful drives in the US!

Route 1 took us right into Monterey Bay where we stopped in to visit the Monterey Bay Aquarium before heading to San Francisco. The Monterey Bay Aquarium is one of the most unique aquariums we've ever visited.

Now, off to San Francisco! This was the highlight of the trip for my teenage daughters. We rode cable cars, visited the cable car museum, went to Lombard street, strolled around Chinatown and visited much of the warf area. We ate sourdough from the Boudin bakery and chocolate from Ghiradelli's. And, of course we had another round of In N Out Burger. We biked across the Golden Gate bridge and got another wonderful view of the Pacific Ocean and San Francisco Bay. One of the surprising things about San Francisco is that even in the summer it can be quite chilly at times. We wore sweatshirts in early summer and we were still cold!

New York City

Duration: 1 Week

After our trip to San Francisco, our daughters clearly had a preference to visit large cities. So, we decided to head to New York City for our next vacation. It was a wonderful opportunity also for my husband and I to return to New York City - not only to see the city but also to visit friends and old haunts. We lived in New Jersey and I worked in New York City before we had kids. It was a little like coming home again. We got to share some of our favorite spots and introduce our daughters to friends we hadn't seen in years. We stayed at a hotel in the Times Square area since it was also very close to a good subway station. The subway is really the way to get around the city. We took a walking tour through Central Park, and visited 5th Aveunue passing by my old workplace next to Rockefeller Center and by the view out my old office window - St. Peter's Cathedral.

On Day 2, we visited the Statue of Liberty, Ellis Island, Chinatown and the 9/11 Museum.

On Day 3, we went to see Wicked on Broadway and had pizza in John's Pizzeria - inside an old church. On Day 4, we ate New York City bagels, went to Grand Central Station, and toured the Empire State Building.

On Day 5, we toured the American Museum of Natural History and the Met Museum. I was able to visit my favorite piece at the Met - a large Tiffany stained glass window. And, in between it all, we got to have dinner with friends.
